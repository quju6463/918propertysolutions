Property options review

Compare the next move
before you commit.

We look at the practical pathways for the same property, so the decision is not based on one optimistic number.

01

Sell as-is

Useful when certainty, speed, privacy or avoiding renovation risk matters more than chasing every last dollar.

02

Renovate then sell

Compare resale uplift against project cost, GST/tax assumptions, selling costs, timing and execution risk.

03

Renovate then rent

Check whether the improved rent, tenant appeal and long-term value justify the spend and holding costs.

04

Short-stay potential

Consider setup cost, occupancy, cleaning, management, council rules, tax and whether the property suits guests.

05

Hold and improve

Review rent range, maintenance, insurance, rates, debt cost and whether the asset still fits the portfolio.

06

Direct purchase

Where suitable, we may consider buying directly. If that applies, we will say clearly that we are a potential purchaser.

How it works

Send the property.
We map the options.

The aim is not to overwhelm you with theory. It is to narrow the decision into a few practical next steps.

1

Send the property details

Address or suburb, photos or listing link, current use, condition, rough numbers and what decision you are trying to make.

2

We compare the pathways

Sale, renovation, rental, short-stay, hold, flip or direct purchase potential, using the assumptions available.

3

You choose the next step

That might be doing nothing, selling, renovating, renting, using the calculators, getting specialist advice, or discussing a direct offer.

Clear interests

We also do property projects ourselves.

That experience helps us think practically about renovation, resale, holding costs and risk. It also means transparency matters. If we are interested in buying, partnering on, or being commercially involved in a property, we will say so clearly. In that situation, we are acting as a potential purchaser or project participant, not as your independent adviser. You should get your own legal, tax, valuation, finance or licensed real estate advice where needed.
